  • Simons Observatory

    Located in the high Atacama Desert in Northern Chile at an altitude of 17,000 ft, the Observatory will provide the best measurements yet of the cosmic microwave background and provide key tests of competing theories the origin of the universe.

    Read More

  • Cosmology with a Bounce

    A targeted grant of the Simons Foundation to support the development of new theories of the origin, evolution, and future of the universe based on replacing the big bang with a bounce, a smooth transition from a previous contracting phase to the current phase of expansion.

  • Numerical General Relativity

    The computational tools invented to predict the gravitational waves generated by merging black holes and neutron stars can be extended to analyze theories of the origin of the universe and determine their predictions.

Science Goal

In a focused effort, we aim to develop full-blown theories of cosmological bounces and bouncing cosmologies and determine their implications for fundamental physics and for astrophysical and cosmological observations.

Research Thrusts

  • Theory of the Bounce

    Develop theories that generate a smooth transition from contraction to expansion

    Numerical GR Simulation

    Design tools of numerical general relativity to simulate bounces and bouncing cosmologies

    Observational Predictions

    Embed bounces into complete cosmological scenarios and identify observational signatures
